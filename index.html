Essay

The majority of people in the U.S. who deal with a mental illness never reach out for help and the number of cases constantly rise.

There are around 1 billion people worldwide suffering from a mental disorder and nearly 280 million people suffering from depression.

280M
depression

About 1 in every 5 adults suffers from any mental illness in the United States, and 1 in every 6 people suffer from depression.

1 in 5 (any mental illness)
1 in 6 (depression)

These are big numbers. They show us that mental illness is not just a person's problem. It happens to families, and in schools, and communities. The pandemic has made this a whole lot worse. To help this we need more real solutions like expanded school and community counseling, increased tele therapy, and more public awareness, so people know how to get help.

One of the major causes of the rise in mental illness was covid19. During the pandemic, many people were in isolation from much of the world. 21% of people reported sever loneliness.

That much time alone is very hard, especially for young kids who are still learning how to deal with stress. Social media was the biggest way that young people stayed in touch with each other. The overuse of social media can make people feel lonely, compare themselves with others, and be exposed to inappropriate content. All this combined led to bigger problems of depression and anxiety.

Social media effects
Qualitative impact

During the pandemic, studies showed a 25% increase in anxiety and depression across the world during the pandemic. When people get depressed for a long time, it can turn into more serious problems like dropping out of school, or even suicidal thoughts. Suicide is now the second highest leading cause of death in ages 10 to 34 in America. All of these facts just show how big this is.

Long-term consequences

Another big issue is that there are many people who don’t receive the help they need. Almost half of all adults in the U.S. that are suffering from mental illness do not get treated.

That is because therapy costs too much or there is no practitioner in their community. One out of every three U.S. counties, actually, has no licensed counselor, psychiatrist, or psychologist. This shows that people in rural or poor communities usually have nowhere to turn.

Provider deserts (approx.)

Also, mental illness reduces people's ability to work and inhibits learning. The economy takes a massive loss, over a trillion dollars each year, because of people not showing up to work or being unable to perform as best they can in their profession. These facts explain how mental illness injures people emotionally while financially impacting society.

$1T+
estimated annual loss
Economic impact (order of magnitude)

A simple solution is to improve school and community counseling services. Schools are where kids are most of the day, so school counselors can catch problems early. If kids are helped early, there is less of a chance they drop out of school, fail courses, or develop later and more complex problems. Community centers can help adults with low-cost or hopefully even free counseling or support groups. When help is near and easy to get, more people will come in early.

We also need to expand telehealth and online counseling. Teletherapy worked for a lot of people during the pandemic. It helps those who are far from a clinic or can’t leave home. Local community centers or maybe libraries can offer free internet and private rooms for people to use for teletherapy. Governments should fund teletherapy so that it is affordable for people. By allowing counselors to practice across state lines would allow for more people to receive help.
